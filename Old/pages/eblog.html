FRC 2015 Entry #19 - Stop Build Day

At 11:45PM PST the Iron Panthers bagged their FRC 2015 Recycle Rush robot, named 4chainz. It lifts and stacks totes, and has an accurate autonomous system, as demonstrated below.

Programming is very satisfied with their code, and engineering is happy with their capture, lift, and stack mechanism.

February 2, 2015

FRC 2015 Entry #18

As we near the last two-week stretch of build season, engineers and programmers are buckling down to grind out the last pieces of the robot. Engineering is finishing up the drive base that they worked on over the weekend. They encountered a problem because they accidentally milled a metal bar incorrectly due to a file error. Due to using the wrong file, the part was misaligned and unable to be used. They are currently in the process of milling the correct part.

Programming is doing well with making progress on the code for the robot. Maggie and Katherine are researching the accelerometer that is built into the roboRIO hardware. Their primary concern is looking up how it works so that they can use it to verify encoder measurements. Peter and Parker are testing the solenoids – today they were able to sync the solenoids with the joystick! Right now they are concentrating on physical testing of the code to make sure everything works well.

The only problem that programming is faced with is getting the motor/gearbox to work. They’re not sure where the problem originates from so they are checking the code to see if there are any errors. Meanwhile, lead programmer Leon is updating the documentation of the codebase to match recent changes, as well as searching for a header-file-only logger class to make debugging the robot post-match easier.

January 21, 2015

FRC 2015 Entry #12

To start off the long work session, engineering worked on continuing to prototype the mechanism for picking up containers. They also separated solenoids for the pneumatics system and cut out clamps for attaching items to the CNC table. Will also began to make a list of field elements for building the practice field. He consulted a SolidWorks design of the field to get precise estimates of materials needed.

Meanwhile, programming concentrated on their individual classes (tasks). These classes contribute to the overall functions and capabilities of the robot. For example, the three motors class allows three motors to be transformed into virtually two for drive. The action map connects every button to its action, and the joystick class determines if a button is pressed up or down. Programming worked diligently and steadily towards constructing the foundation code for the robot.

Business continued to make progress on their various projects and awards. The film/video team also worked on their outline for the Chairman’s Award Video and Robot Release Video. Please stay tuned for more updates and, in the next few weeks, our videos!

January 14, 2015

FRC 2015 Entry #8

Wednesday late night sessions: intense, productive, and energetic.

Today, programming worked on testing the SmartDashboard on the roboRIO along with beginning efforts on the JoystickWrapper, EventRelay, Logger, Button, and Action classes designed by Peter and Leon. Early on, both programmers and engineers ran into a problem with the electronics test bed when a ferrule connector got stuck in a Weidmuller connector of the Power Distribution Panel. January 6, 2015

FRC 2015 Entry #1

The last few days of winter break were busy for the Iron Panthers. Following kickoff at Woodside High, the engineers and programmers shuttled off to the house of Engineering Lead, Reuben Grossman to read and discuss the rules. Reading each and every section of the game manual out loud, the team made sure to acquaint themselves with the specifics of the game so they could be prepared for competitions this season.

Monday was another day off for BHS students, but not for the Iron Panthers! Engineers and programmers met to review the rules once more and start discussing the merits of various strategies. They concurred that moving the totes would be the key part of the robot’s functions. On the other hand, using a cost benefit analysis, they reached the conclusion that picking up the pool noodles “litter” would not be advantageous.

Today is Tuesday, and the team is beginning to start developing a game plan for tackling Recycle Rush. Programmers are outlining how they are going to design and allocate the code, as well as coordinate which team members will work on specific systems. Engineers are making sure that all members have read the rules.
